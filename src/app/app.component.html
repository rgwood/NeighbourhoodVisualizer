Setback Visualizer

Urban planners use a lot of policy tools to shape our built environment, and one of the big ones is setback requirements. In a nutshell, this means requiring that buildings be set back from the property lines to provide space for yards or other uses. This tool is intended to help visualize those requirements.

By default, this shows the setback requirements from Vancouver's RS-1 "One-Family Dwelling" zone. This zone covers most of the residential land in Vancouver. There are some minor exceptions, but generally RS-1 lots require a front yard that is 20% of the lot depth, side yards that are 10% of the lot width, and a back yard that is 45% (!) of the lot depth. After all that, there is not a huge amount of land left to build on.
